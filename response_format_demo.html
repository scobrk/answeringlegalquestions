Direct Answer

For the 2024-25 financial year, NSW payroll tax applies at a rate of 5.45% on taxable wages above the threshold of $1.2 million. Businesses with total Australian wages below this threshold are exempt from payroll tax obligations.

Supporting Details

Calculation Method: Payroll tax is calculated monthly on the total taxable wages paid during that month, applied at the rate of 5.45%.

Grouping Provisions: Related entities must combine their wages when determining if the threshold is exceeded. This includes subsidiaries, associated companies, and entities under common control.

Annual vs Monthly Returns: Businesses can apply for annual return arrangements if their estimated annual liability is under $20,000, allowing payment once per year instead of monthly.
